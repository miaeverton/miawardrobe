# FOOTWEAR PLAYBOOK
## Mia's Outfits Project — Complete Shoe Decision Reference

---

## THE CORE PRINCIPLE

The shoe controls the occasion. Not the clothes. The same outfit — black tube top, light wash wide-leg jeans — reads casual with a flat sandal, smart casual with a ballet flat, and evening with a kitten heel slingback. The clothes set the palette. The shoe sets the occasion.

Apply this to every outfit. Always.

---

## ALISON BORNSTEIN FRAMEWORK (apply to every shoe decision)

1. **Leg line** — What does this shoe do to the leg? Pointed toe elongates. Round toe shortens. Chunky sole adds visual weight at the base.
2. **Balance** — Does the shoe balance the outfit's weight? Heavy/voluminous top → sleek minimal shoe. Minimal outfit → interesting shoe creates the moment.
3. **Wrong-shoe move** — Is there an unexpected shoe choice that creates personality without breaking the look?
4. **Two options always** — identify a primary shoe and one alternative. Explain which is stronger and why.
5. **Flats are choices** — justify them as deliberate, never as defaults.

## BODY-SPECIFIC FOOTWEAR LOGIC

Mia is tall with a strong, proportionate hourglass frame.

- Flat shoes do not shorten her — her height absorbs them
- A kitten heel provides exactly the right amount of elongation without reading "dressed up"
- Wide-leg trousers + pointed flat = an unbroken vertical line that is more powerful on a tall frame
- Chunky soles and ankle straps interrupt the vertical line — avoid
- The ankle strap specifically cuts the leg at a visible point — avoid with wide-leg trousers
- Platform heels appear once in the lookbooks (party/evening board only) — not a daily option

## THE WRONG-SHOE THEORY IN PRACTICE

The wrong-shoe move must meet three conditions:
1. The shoe is the only casual element — the rest of the outfit is polished
2. The contrast is intentional, not accidental
3. The shoe is minimal (not loud) — the contrast is the silhouette mismatch, not the shoe itself

**Validated wrong-shoe moves (from lookbooks):**
- Black Chuck Taylor + black satin maxi skirt (WIN-02)
- White sneaker + all-black look with oversized shirt (SPR-05)
- White Chuck Taylor + white button-down + black maxi skirt (WIN-10)
- Onitsuka Tiger + black T-shirt maxi dress (SPR-45)

**New wrong-shoe moves (AB-identified gaps, now validated):**
- Oxford/penny loafer + ivory ruched maxi dress (E07)
- Cream penny loafer + black puff-sleeve Doen midi dress (E10)

## SPECIFIC SHOE NOTES FROM THIS WARDROBE

**SHO-004 — Woven Bow Flat (Freida Salvador)**
Brand confirmed by Mia. Tan, woven, bow detail, square toe. Not a typical ballet flat — the square toe reads slightly more modern. Pairs well with light wash jeans, white skirts, and orange/terra cotta dresses.

**SHO-007 — Cream/White Penny Loafer**
The AB loafer. Most useful as the wrong-shoe move with dresses. Reads borrowed-from-menswear in a way ballet flats don't. Use with the ivory maxi dress, the Doen midi dress, and dark indigo jeans.

**SHO-010 — Black Oxford Loafer**
Same function as SHO-007 but darker and more structured. Use with silk trousers, chunky knits, and dark-palette outfits where the cream loafer would be too light.

**SHO-012 — White Strappy Flat Sandal (Reformation)**
Confirmed as a strappy flat sandal — not a kitten heel. Works for summer casual and vacation.

**SHO-014 — Snakeskin Slide Sandal**
Confirmed as a flat slide — not a kitten heel. Flat, strappy, snakeskin. Treat as a neutral slide.

**SHO-028 — Snakeskin Slingback Kitten Heel**
PARTIAL CROP — only toe and strap visible. Do not feature in outfit boards until re-cropped. Lower confidence (70%).

## WHAT TO AVOID

- Platform heels in everyday contexts
- Boots with skirts (never appears in lookbooks — not an established move)
- Literal shoe-to-bag matching (echo the palette, don't replicate it)
- Ankle-strap sandals with wide-leg trousers (cuts the leg line)
- Chunky soles as everyday footwear
- Recommending a flat as a "safe choice" — always frame flats as the right choice

## SHOE OPTIONS BY OUTFIT CONTEXT

### With wide-leg jeans (light wash)
- Primary: ballet flat or woven flat (daytime)
- Elevate to evening: kitten heel slingback
- Wrong-shoe: high-top Chuck Taylor or low-top sneaker
- AB gap: penny loafer for more creative register

### With wide-leg jeans (dark indigo)
- Primary: pointed ballet flat or slingback flat
- Elevate to evening: snakeskin kitten heel slingback
- Wrong-shoe: low-top sneaker

### With black/brown silk maxi skirt
- Primary: black kitten heel slingback (evening)
- Daytime: black pointed flat or mary jane
- Wrong-shoe: Chuck Taylor (validated in WIN-02)

### With dresses (maxi/midi)
- T-shirt maxi: any shoe — shoe controls everything
- Ivory ruched maxi: loafer (AB move), heeled sandal, or kitten heel
- Colour dress: flat sandal, woven flat, or natural sandal only
- Doen puff-sleeve midi: penny loafer (AB wrong-shoe — the defining move)

### With blazer outfits
- Work/smart casual: pointed ballet flat or slingback flat
- Evening: kitten heel slingback or snakeskin slingback
- Wrong-shoe: high-top Chuck Taylor (validated in SPR-22)
